## 🛡️ Cash strategy update: CBIL vs aggregate bond ETFs

Instead of holding idle cash in **aggregate bond ETFs** (e.g., ZAG, VAB, XBB) as mentioned in the previous [post](https://dropinanocean.github.io/finance/2025/12/19/Finance-15.html), I pivoted to **[CBIL](https://www.globalx.ca/product/cbil)** — a short-term (0–3 month) Government of Canada bond ETF.

- **Why CBIL:** removes most interest-rate risk.
- **Aggregate bond behavior:**
  - Rates ↓ (BoC easing / recession): aggregate bond ETFs generally rise.
  - Rates ↑ (inflationary pressure / oil shock): aggregate bond ETFs can fall.

This is a tactical move to keep safer liquidity available for opportunities.
