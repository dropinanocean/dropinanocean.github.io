### Summary
<em>Good Company</em> is a business and leadership memoir from Arthur M. Blank, co-founder of The Home Depot and owner of the Atlanta Falcons, Atlanta United, and Mercedes-Benz Stadium. Featuring an introduction by President Jimmy Carter, the book argues that purpose and profit are not at odds, and that values-driven leadership is the real engine behind lasting business success.

### What the book is about
Blank left The Home Depot in 2001 with a question on his mind: could the values and culture that helped build a $50 billion retailer be replicated elsewhere? Good Company answers that question by taking readers through his efforts to turn around a struggling NFL franchise, rebuild a near-bankrupt retail chain, construct a brand-new stadium, help revitalize an underserved Atlanta neighborhood, and launch a Major League Soccer team from scratch.

Along the way, Blank lays out the core values that guided him and cofounder Bernie Marcus at Home Depot, from caring for associates and customers to giving back to the communities they operated in. He makes the case that when companies put the wellbeing of employees, customers, and communities first, financial results follow, rather than the other way around. The book blends personal history with practical leadership lessons on culture, hiring, risk, and growth, offering a roadmap for anyone trying to build an organization where doing right by people and doing well financially go hand in hand.
